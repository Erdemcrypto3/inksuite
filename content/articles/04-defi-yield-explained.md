# Where Does DeFi Yield Come From? (And Is It Real?)

"Deposit your tokens and earn 8% APY." Sounds great, right? But if you can't explain where that yield comes from, you probably shouldn't be putting money into it.

## The Three Real Sources of Yield

Not all DeFi yield is created equal. There are basically three legitimate ways protocols generate returns:

### 1. Lending Interest

The simplest one. Protocols like Tydro (on Ink) or Aave let you deposit assets. Borrowers pay interest to use those assets. You get a cut.

It's the same model as a bank savings account, except there's no bank taking most of the spread. Interest rates float based on supply and demand — if lots of people want to borrow ETH, the rate goes up. If nobody's borrowing, it drops.

**Risk level:** Relatively low. Smart contract risk exists, but the model itself is sound.

### 2. Trading Fees

When you provide liquidity to a DEX (like Velodrome on Ink), you're essentially letting traders use your tokens. Every time someone swaps through your pool, you earn a small fee — usually 0.3% of the trade.

High-volume pairs generate solid returns. Low-volume pairs sit there doing nothing. And there's impermanent loss to think about — if the price ratio of your two tokens changes significantly, you might end up with less than if you'd just held.

**Risk level:** Medium. Impermanent loss is real but often overstated for stable pairs.

### 3. Token Incentives

This is where it gets tricky. Protocols often pay extra rewards in their own token to attract liquidity. "Earn 40% APY in PROTOCOL_TOKEN!" Sounds amazing until you realize the token is dropping 50% per month because everyone is farming and dumping it.

Token incentives aren't inherently bad — they bootstrap liquidity. But they're not sustainable forever. The best protocols use incentives to get started, then transition to real fee revenue.

**Risk level:** High if you don't understand the tokenomics.

## Red Flags to Watch

If someone promises any of these, walk away:

- **"Guaranteed" returns.** Nothing in DeFi is guaranteed. Not even the big protocols.
- **APYs over 100% with no explanation.** Where's the money coming from? If the answer is "new depositors," that's a Ponzi structure.
- **Locked tokens with no unlock schedule.** If you can't exit, that's not a feature — it's a trap.
- **Anonymous team with no track record.** Plenty of legit anon builders exist, but combine anon with suspicious tokenomics and you've got trouble.

## A Practical Framework

Before depositing anywhere, ask yourself:

1. **What am I providing?** (Lending capital? Liquidity? Something else?)
2. **Who is paying me?** (Borrowers? Traders? The protocol's token printer?)
3. **What can go wrong?** (Smart contract hack? Depeg? Token dump?)
4. **Can I exit when I want?** (Lockups? Withdrawal delays?)

If you can answer all four clearly, you probably understand the risk. If you can't, keep learning before you deploy capital.

## The Bottom Line

DeFi yield is real, but it's not free money. It's compensation for taking risk — smart contract risk, market risk, liquidity risk. The higher the yield, the higher the risk. Always.

Start with simple lending on established protocols. Earn your 3-5%, learn how everything works, and scale up as your understanding grows.
