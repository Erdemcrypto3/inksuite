# What is Ink? Kraken's Bet on Making DeFi Actually Usable

If you've been anywhere near crypto Twitter lately, you've probably seen "Ink" popping up. So what is it, and why should you care?

## The Short Version

Ink is a Layer 2 blockchain built by Kraken — yes, the exchange with 10+ million users. It launched in December 2024, ahead of schedule, and it's built on Optimism's OP Stack (the same tech behind Base, which Coinbase built).

The pitch is simple: DeFi is powerful but most people find it terrifying. Kraken wants to change that by giving their massive user base a smooth on-ramp into decentralized finance.

## What Can You Actually Do on Ink?

Right now, the ecosystem is small but growing fast. The big three:

**Tydro** — A lending and borrowing protocol (basically Aave, but on Ink). It's already sitting at over $400M in TVL, which is wild for a chain this young. You deposit assets, earn yield, or borrow against your holdings.

**Nado** — A perpetual DEX built in-house by Kraken. Think decentralized leverage trading with an order book. It's pulling about $40M in TVL and generating serious revenue.

**Velodrome** — The DEX that followed from Optimism. Liquidity pools, yield farming, the usual suspects. Around $14M locked.

## The Numbers That Matter

Here's what caught my attention: Ink's TVL went from $7 million in October 2025 to nearly $450 million by early 2026. That's not a typo. App revenue jumped from $500K to $5.77M per month in the same period.

Gas fees? Basically nothing. We're talking fractions of a cent per transaction. That alone makes it worth experimenting with.

## The Token Situation

The Ink Foundation introduced the INK token in mid-2025, with a TGE (Token Generation Event) expected in Q2-Q3 2026. If you're building or using things on Ink right now, you're probably accumulating some kind of position — though nothing is guaranteed.

There's also the Spark Program for builders (grants from $500 to $5,000) and the Forge Program for projects that show real traction (up to $200K).

## Why It Might Actually Work

Most L2s launch and then scramble to find users. Ink has something different: Kraken's existing user base. If even 5% of Kraken's users bridge over and start poking around DeFi for the first time, that's hundreds of thousands of new users.

The chain is also part of Optimism's Superchain, which means cross-chain composability is baked in. Assets and messages can flow between Ink, Base, Optimism, and other Superchain networks.

## My Take

Is Ink going to "kill Ethereum"? No, and it's not trying to. It's trying to be the place where normal people finally start using DeFi without needing a PhD in smart contracts. And with Kraken's resources behind it, they've got a real shot.

Whether you're a builder looking for grants or just someone who wants to try DeFi without spending $50 on gas, Ink is worth a look.
